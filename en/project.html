The project

Wastewater harvesting and treatment.

The Chimborazo: a volcano at 6,263 meters. This is the highest point in Ecuador.

Our goals

The aim of our project is to harvest and treat wastewaters from the El Tejar community in the Chimborazo province of Ecuador. Being an area where it does not rain much, it is essential to manage the water well. Therefore, one of the objectives of the project will be to sensitize the community to the water management, to a better use of the natural resources but especially to the protection of the water resources. We work in collaboration with the Universidad Nacional de Chimborazo and the Pastoral Social Cáritas Riobamba.

Our motivation

We are aware that the water crisis hangs in our face and that water is a key element for development. We believe that the problem is central to the proper development of a society and therefore of a community. Thus, the goal is clearly drawn. In addition to the learning dimension and the personal experience, there is a human dimension of mutual help - from us to the local population but also from the local population towards us - that will stimulate the team and make the project a real life experience. .
